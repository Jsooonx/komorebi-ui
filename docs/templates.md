# Website Template Prompt Library

The Template Showcase (located in Page 4) features 8 premium landing page templates built for rapid deployment. It acts as an AI prompt library, enabling developers to build production-grade animated interfaces with a single prompt.

---

## 🎨 Visual Assets & Deferrals

- **Aura AI Video Mockup:** The Aura B2B AI landing page (`aura`) features a 4MB MP4 video mockup. The video tag is set to `preload="none"` to block the browser from loading video files on the initial load, saving bandwidth and reducing initial CPU layout recalculations.
- **Smooth Hover Playhead Reset:** When a developer hovers over the Aura template card, the video starts playing from frame 0 (`videoRef.current.currentTime = 0; videoRef.current.play()`). This is handled via a ref pointer rather than unmounting and remounting react keys, removing black frame flashing.
- **Aura Resolution Relay:** The section after Aura's hero translates one incoming customer signal through intent, context, and resolution. Its warm forest, sunlight, and soft-aqua palette extends Aura's hero image rather than introducing a disconnected dark dashboard. An original generated aqua-glass artwork acts as the resolution visual, while the header, signal, stage controls, detail, and outcome enter in a single hero-style staggered sequence; the interaction remains a native motion-driven relay instead of a browser mockup or fake chat interface.
- **Aura Seamless Handoff:** Aura's third section demonstrates human continuity rather than another automated reply. An original generated wide artwork depicts two glass forms connected by one unbroken aqua signal, reinforcing that intent, history, urgency, and a suggested next action travel intact when a specialist takes over. The three context records enter individually in sequence, mirroring how a handoff record is assembled.
- **Aura Connected Context:** Aura's fourth section explains where an informed answer comes from. A generated forest-gold visual shows five distinct glass source nodes converging into an aqua Aura context core. Context Trace upgrades each local hover/focus into a radial lens, immediately drawn source-to-core path, one-shot travelling pulse, core light-up, and a morphing `record attached` label—avoiding a generic integrations logo wall. New traces no longer wait for the prior trace to exit, and the active hover surface now morphs spatially toward the next source rather than flashing in place.
- **Aura Guidance Layer:** Aura's fifth section turns voice, knowledge boundaries, and escalation safeguards into three glass gates along one continuous aqua current. A single understated connection path crosses the natural section boundary from the closing Connected Context copy into the Guidance heading, so the relationship reads without a standalone transition, divider, label, or extra visual element. Hovering or focusing a gate immediately draws the local trace, brings that gate forward, and morphs the supporting explanation—keeping the interaction calm, legible, and distinct from a settings dashboard. A shared active surface now travels between gates in the actual hover direction. Its lower content is an editorial operating standard with three compact principles, rather than a generic paired-detail layout. The forest-gold editorial artwork is an original generated asset.
- **Aura Anticipation Loop:** Aura's sixth section closes the product narrative with proactive support rather than another operational surface. A generated forest-path visual shows one aqua current reaching three early customer moments—friction noticed, guidance arriving, and momentum protected. Hover/focus immediately traces the current and morphs the explanation inside the visual; below it, a three-step outcome rail shows the complete proactive sequence. Its active highlight also travels between the spatial markers rather than flashing. This intentionally differs from Guidance Layer's editorial standard note.
- **Aura Quiet Launch:** Aura's seventh section is the narrative close: one calm glass threshold carries the signal into a first customer conversation. Three local proof marks—trusted knowledge, prepared handoff, and a ready first channel—light up independently on hover/focus, while the only persistent action is a simple first-conversation CTA. The generated sunrise clearing keeps the ending optimistic and human rather than turning it into a launch checklist or dashboard.
- **Aura Footer:** Aura ends with a deliberately quiet deep-forest footer: one short brand statement, compact Product and Company utility links, a single demo action, and a low-contrast legal/status row. It uses one restrained in-view reveal only—no additional visual, hover narrative, or text cycling after the closing CTA.
- **Aura Gallery Metadata:** The Aura template card lists `7 Sections`: Hero, Resolution Relay, Seamless Handoff, Connected Context, Guidance Layer, Anticipation Loop, and Quiet Launch. The footer is intentionally excluded from this content-section count.
- **Vellum Rebrand:** `references/templates/Template-Rebrand/vellum/` is a standalone full rebrand informed by, but isolated from, `Lovable-Design/Pallet-Ross`. It replaces the original marketplace language with a private contemporary-editions platform and has six surfaces: Hero, Selected Editions, Collector's Lens, Artist Notes, Private Viewing, and Footer. Its original generated gallery imagery lives under `public/assets/vellum/artwork/`; the source reference remains unchanged.
- **Vellum Interaction Refinement:** Collector's Lens uses a fixed detail slot and uniform row geometry so its shared hover highlight changes content without shifting controls or clipping the oversized index. Box CTAs invert to black locally; underline CTAs draw a black left-to-right cover line over their resting rule.
- **Vellum Entrances:** Vellum uses restrained, one-time staggered entrances across its navigation, hero, editions, lens, notes, viewing room, and footer. Content enters in reading order, while the existing hover and selection behavior remains independent and does not replay after entry.
- **Vellum Gallery Card:** Vellum replaces the former Jplus entry as the first Template Showcase card. Its supplied `public/assets/templates/vellum/preview.mp4` is the card preview and restarts cleanly on hover, matching the existing gallery-video behavior. Selecting the card opens the live Vellum preview at `https://vellum-komorebi.vercel.app/`; `VITE_VELLUM_URL` provides the local environment override.
- **Vesper Rebrand:** `references/templates/Template-Rebrand/vesper/` is a standalone full rebrand of `Lovable-Design/Pallet-Ross`, retaining the high-fidelity scroll card interaction in a dark-mode obsidian, warm brass, and ivory theme. It includes custom generated brutalist architectural images, material annotation tags (#travertine, #raw-bronze), and a new `/collection` route displaying specifications for seven raw materials.
- **Serein and Vesper Gallery Cards:** Serein and Vesper are live Template Showcase entries, each using its supplied preview video. Their cards open the shared Komorebi preview shell and preserve the Gallery scroll position on return. `VITE_SEREIN_URL` and `VITE_VESPER_URL` provide deploy URL overrides, defaulting to `https://serein-komorebi.vercel.app/` and `https://vesper-komorebi.vercel.app/`.
- **Template Preview Chrome:** Every live template preview uses the Aura-established Komorebi shell: the same Sunlight Leaf loading mark and pulse on an obsidian overlay, warm-cream `Back to Gallery` control at top-left, and warm-cream `Komorebi UI` watermark at bottom-right. Template content stays independent; loading, navigation, and attribution chrome remain consistent for all current and future previews.
- **Template Identity Assets:** Aura and Vellum each expose a dedicated root-level SVG favicon with an explicit shortcut-icon fallback and versioned URL, so browser tabs use the template's actual identity rather than a blank, inherited, or cached application icon.
- **Coming Soon Placeholders:** 5 templates (`shyen`, `synergeus`, `aeon`, `apex`, `kora`) display a stylized "Coming Soon / In Queue" dashboard placeholder overlay, indicating templates scheduled for upcoming releases.

## 📋 Copy Prompt Sharing

1.  **Prompt Storage:** The structured prompts containing typography specifications, layout structures, HSL tokens, and animation directives are stored in the templates data list (`TemplateShowcase.tsx`).
2.  **Clipboard Copying:** Clicking "Copy Prompt" on any card copies the string to the user's clipboard and triggers a toast notification via `sonner`. Developers can paste this prompt directly into LLM builders like Claude, Lovable, or v0 to generate full page templates in seconds.

## Aura mobile composition

Aura has a native narrow-screen composition rather than a scaled desktop artboard. The desktop minimum width is removed; generated artwork becomes a compact visual stage; and the spatial controls in Connected Context, Guidance Layer, Anticipation Loop, and Quiet Launch become readable tap lists below their artwork. This preserves local trace interactions without cropped, overlapping labels or horizontal overflow.
